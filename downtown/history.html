History of Downtown Charlottesville

The Charlottesville Downtown Mall Historic District, spanning eight city blocks, is a pedestrianized segment of Main Street located in the heart of the city's commercial area. Completed in two phases between 1976 and 1980, it showcases the urban design vision of landscape architect Lawrence Halprin. The Downtown Mall is notable for its focus on pedestrian movement, incorporating elements such as fountains, seating, and public artworks. As the only pedestrian-only portion of Main Street in Virginia, it stands as a significant example of 20th-century urban revitalization and remains an important part of Charlottesville's historical landscape.
